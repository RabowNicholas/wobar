# WOBAR PLACEMENT PLAN

## North Star
**People listening. Audience growing.** Not playlist adds or a label logo as trophies — actual listeners. Every lever is judged by whether it grows the count of people hearing Wobar. A tactic that doesn't move that gets cut.

## Operating Constraints (locked 2026-06-18)
- **Time:** a few hours/week on placement, on top of make + ship → must be systematized (templated, batched, per-release).
- **Paid budget:** <$50/mo → minimal paid; mostly organic/relationship. Paid is a test, not the engine.
- **Solo.** Every recurring action fits the release packet or it won't happen.

## Exclusivity Decision (locked — option C extended)
- **Flips + remixes** → self-release (SoundCloud), feed the social/curator machine.
- **Originals** → default self-release (DistroKid → Spotify), BUT tag the strongest as **"reserve"** — held unreleased for label demos.
- **Reserve format:** decide by quality later (single vs Portal EP). Tag reserves as made.
- **Critical reason this matters:** the open-door labels below (Gravitas especially) require **unreleased** material. If everything ships, there's nothing to submit. The reserve rule is what keeps the label lever alive.

**Verified (3-0):** Denver is the global bass-music epicenter; **sub.mission is the scene heart**. Two of the five target labels — **Memory Palace (Of The Trees)** and **Local Void (INZO)** — are Denver labels run by two of Wobar's three core sonic comps.

→ **The promoter/scene lever IS the label lever** for the two best targets. Showing up in the Denver corridor (shows, support slots, in-person A&R, scene relationships) is the real path into Memory Palace — not a demo email. The SLC→Denver corridor focus is the single highest-leverage bet in this whole plan.

## Promo Services — verdicts

### Intellijend — SKIP the service, STEAL the playbook
- Hype mechanism (secret "popularity score" via API, ad-spike thresholds) **refuted 0-3** — marketing fiction.
- Real cost ≈ $25 sub + $10-20/day Meta spend ≈ **~$325/mo** — over budget.
- **DIY playbook (within budget):** run own Meta ads → a pre-save / Spotify link, **$5-10/day**, target **lookalike audiences of Of The Trees / INZO / Shlump** listeners. That's the entire trick.
- **⚠ Kill-switch (2026-07-23):** ad traffic is only safe if it lands and *saves/completes*. Ad-driven listeners who stream but don't save/complete produce **incoherent on-platform signal that actively suppresses algorithmic pickup** — cheap broad reach is negative, not neutral. So keep the tight lookalike-of-comps targeting (that's the coherent version) and **judge each ad cohort by S4A save/completion, not clicks or raw streams.** If an audience streams but doesn't convert → kill it fast; it's teaching the recommender the wrong people. Never broaden past aligned lookalikes for cheaper reach.

### Magic Nothing — legit, use the free tier
- Education-first marketing platform (Zack Fischmann), well-reviewed.
- **Free playlist-credit system** (curate others → earn credits → pitch own) fits the $0 lane.
- One-time $180 content workshop optional — skills investment, not recurring.

## Phase 1 — Three Pipelines

### Curators + DIY ads (PRIMARY)
- Editorial pitch = per-release action via S4A, 7+ days pre-release → bolt onto packet T-21/T-14.
- **Pitch metadata is real recommender input now** (not just editor-read — Semantic-ID work). Fill EVERY field maximally specific per release: exact subgenre, BPM, half-time/full, mood, instrumentation ("melodic dubstep, 140 BPM, half-time drums, detuned supersaw, euphoric" — never "electronic"). Costs nothing, direct leverage. Packet checklist item = "metadata fully specified," not just "pitch sent."
- DIY Meta ads: $5-10/day to pre-save/Spotify link, lookalikes of core comps. Test per release. **Govern by the kill-switch above** (save/completion, not streams).
- **CTA asks for FOLLOW first, save second.** The only hard guaranteed mechanic in the system: pitch ≥7 days out → guaranteed Release Radar reach to existing followers. Followers = a compounding guaranteed floor every release; a stream is one-time. Ad + social + pre-save CTAs prioritize the follow.
- Magic Nothing free playlist credits as the no-cost curator channel.

## Metrics (judged against the north star)
- Primary (audience): monthly listeners ↑, Spotify followers ↑ (compounding RR floor), saves, streams/30d.
- **Source of Streams % (the ground-truth instrument, per release):** track **algorithmic share** vs editorial vs listener vs profile in S4A. It's the one number that says whether the recommender picked you up. Reads:
  - algo share <10-15% *with* healthy saves/completion = **reach** problem → widen aligned niche playlisting + tighten cohort seeding.
  - weak saves/completion = **fit/targeting** problem → do NOT pay to amplify (you'd be paying to teach the algorithm the wrong audience).
- Secondary (lever health): playlist adds, demo responses, bookings/support slots.
- Rule: a lever not moving primary metrics after a fair test gets de-prioritized.
