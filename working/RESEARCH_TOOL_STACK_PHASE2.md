# Phase 2 — Stage-Appropriate Comp Artists

## Selection rationale

The original AI doc listed Of The Trees, INZO, LSDREAM, and Shlump — all four are 500K-1M+ monthly listener artists with established label, festival, and management infrastructure. Their playbooks (Of The Trees' Jan 2026 social wipe rollout, INZO's 100M-stream catalog leverage, LSDREAM's full A&R team) are not portable to a zero-audience act dropping his first releases. They're aspirational, not actionable.

Phase 2 surfaces six artists in or near the 5K–75K monthly Spotify listener band, all active in 2024–2026, all in adjacent bass lanes (experimental/freeform/psychedelic/halftime/wonk), all with documented online presence we can actually study. They cluster into three useful buckets:

- **Just-broken (50K-ish, 3-4 years deep, label-anchored):** Mport (50.5K), Acyan (54.6K) — what "having broken through" looks like, and the platforms that got them there.
- **Breaking-now (10K-35K, 1-2 years deep, mid-tier label or scrappy):** AYCH (32.5K), CANVAS (12.2K) — Wakaan / Bassrush / Subsidia signings happening in real time.
- **Genuinely emerging (5K-10K, blog-discovered, no major label):** Kaiyo (9.2K), Luhv (6.9K) — closest comp to Wobar's actual starting point.

**Dropped from candidate list:**
- Tape B (~1M+), Wreckno (207K), Hairitage (326K), Canabliss (103.8K), Casey Club (119.9K), SICARIA (77.8K) — all above the 75K ceiling.
- Khiva (28.9K, in-range): debuted on DD&D in 2017, so her playbook is a 9-year arc, not a 1-3 year trajectory.
- Conrank (37.2K), Detox Unit (24.8K): both ~10+ years into their projects, not "recently emerging."
- Toadface, Tripp St., Tsuruda: all started 2019–2021, project arcs too old.
- Bandlez, Daily Bread, Squnto (4.3K): legacy projects that have either grown well beyond stage-appropriate or shrunk below visible-footprint threshold.
- JAWNSIN (2.5K monthly listeners, KC dubstep, FUXWITHIT Oct 2025 interview): great editorial fit, below the 5K floor — keep on the watchlist.
- Yancey (47 monthly listeners): too small to study a playbook.

## Cross-Artist Patterns

### Content + cadence patterns

- **Release cadence > social cadence.** Across all six, output frequency on Spotify/SoundCloud appears to do more lifting than IG/TikTok post volume. Kaiyo (9.2K listeners, 97 IG posts, song-a-week) and Acyan (54.6K listeners, 46 IG posts) both have small social footprints relative to listener counts. Only **Mport** has invested heavily in social-as-channel (1,035 IG posts, 26.8K TikTok), and his TikTok is humor-forward, not production-process.
- **Flips and remixes of recognizable IP are a recurring breakout lever.** Mport (Beatles "Day Tripper"), AYCH (Mac Miller "Nike's On My Feet"), Kaiyo (Tove Lo "Habits," Kendrick "Swimming Pools," Zedd "Clarity"). Three of six. The flip provides built-in search/discovery momentum that an original cannot.
- **Nobody is on every platform.** Each artist seems to pick one anchor and dominate it. Mport → TikTok. Acyan → SoundCloud + co-sign network. AYCH → SoundCloud flips. Kaiyo → SoundCloud + FUXWITHIT. CANVAS → local-scene IRL + Wakaan. Luhv → FUXWITHIT.
- **SoundCloud is the actual incubator.** Five of six use SoundCloud as their primary upload-first surface. Spotify is downstream of SoundCloud traction, not the reverse. This aligns with the Phase 1 finding that SoundCloud #bassline tag tracks are up 37% over 2 years.

### Label / blog patterns

- **The blogs that are actually covering this tier are concentrated.** Across all six profiles, the consistent press footprint is:
  - **FUXWITHIT** (3 of 6: AYCH, Kaiyo, Luhv) — by far the most-active outlet at this stage.
  - **EDM Identity** (3 of 6: AYCH, Acyan, CANVAS) — Artist Spotlight series is the gateway format.
  - **Electric Hawk** (2 of 6: Acyan listed in 2025 watch list; surfaces elsewhere).
  - **Stage Hoppers** (Mport, others).
  - **UKF** (Acyan "Ones to Watch") — UK-skewed, more for 140 / UKG.
  - **Rooster Magazine** (Mport) — Denver-local, scene-specific.

  Notably absent or weakly present: The Untz, Run The Trap (some legacy coverage, less relevant 2024+), Magnetic Mag (occasional), Insomniac (artist pages but no breakout coverage). The Phase 1 doc's implication that "EDM blogs" is a single addressable channel doesn't match what's actually opening doors — **FUXWITHIT + EDM Identity is doing most of the work at this stage.**

- **Most accessible labels at this stage (based on which signed these artists in 2023-2025):**
  - **Bassrush Records** (AYCH original)
  - **Subsidia** (Excision's label, AYCH debut)
  - **Gravitas** (AYCH)
  - **FUXWITHIT** (label arm — Luhv debut, KUREI collab)
  - **Sable Valley** (compilation placement for Acyan)
  - **MONTA, Alter/Ego** (Acyan)
  - **Wakaan** (Mport via collab 2022, CANVAS solo debut 2025) — possible but takes 4+ years of scene-building first
  - **Wetware Records, Realm Records** (JAWNSIN — smaller home)

  **Pattern: every artist in the set landed at least one release on a label they didn't found themselves, but none have an exclusive deal.** Multi-label scattering is the norm at this stage.

### Tool / platform patterns

- **Hypeddit smart links — confirmed on at least one artist (Mport, hypeddit.com/mport/daytripper).** Not visibly on the surface for the others, but that's about visibility, not necessarily absence.
- **Bandcamp store presence — confirmed for Mport (mport.bandcamp.com).** This matters because Bandcamp is the only platform on this list that pays the artist directly per sale.
- **Agency representation arrives around 30K+ monthly listeners.** Acyan (Worm Agency, 54.6K), AYCH (Corson Agency, 32.5K). Below 30K, artists are self-booking. **Implication: Wobar doesn't need an agent yet — but the threshold is visible.**
- **No visible evidence of Patreon, Substack, or MailerLite footers** on any of the six surface profiles. This is either because (a) they're not using them, or (b) email/subscription tools live one layer deeper than the social surface and weren't surfaced by this research pass. **This is a real gap worth investigating before Phase 3.**
- **Linktree/Beacons-style aggregators** mentioned only in passing (FUXWITHIT itself uses one). Most artists go direct from IG bio to Spotify or to a Bandcamp.

### Time-to-10K patterns

Hard to verify precisely (these are present-day snapshots, not historical curves), but reasonable inferences:

- **Acyan**: 2021 SoundCloud start → ~54.6K by mid-2026. ~5 years to 50K. Probably hit 10K somewhere in the 18-30 month range based on EDM Identity Spotlight timing (Sept 2023 = 2 years in, already enough traction to merit a spotlight).
- **AYCH**: 2021 AYCH alias debut → ~32.5K by mid-2026. ~5 years to 32K. SoundCloud flips driving early growth.
- **Mport**: 2017 first co-sign → 2022 Wakaan collab → ~50.5K by 2026. The TVBOO bus tour (2024) appears to be the slope-change moment.
- **Kaiyo**: 2023 first FUXWITHIT mention → ~9.2K by mid-2026. **~2.5 years to 9K, dropping a song a week.** This is the closest-to-Wobar trajectory in the set.
- **CANVAS**: 2021 active → Wakaan debut Nov 2025 → 12.2K. ~4 years to 12K. Patience model.
- **Luhv**: 2024 FUXWITHIT label debut → 6.9K. ~1.5 years to 7K with concentrated label backing.

**Inferred median path: 18-30 months of consistent release output + flips + one tastemaker blog champion to cross 10K monthly listeners.** This is much slower than the Phase 1 doc implied with its Hypeddit/SubmitHub conversion math. The 10K threshold is roughly 2 years of work, not 2 quarters.

### Anything that contradicts the original AI doc's strategy

1. **The "playlist push + smart link + email list" stack is not visibly present at this stage.** None of the six profiles surfaced a MailerLite/Substack newsletter. The original doc implied email is essential from day one; at the 5K-50K listener band, **email appears to be a later-stage tool**, deployed after listener count justifies it.

2. **"Of The Trees / INZO" comp playbooks are wrong-scale.** Of The Trees' Jan 2026 social wipe + cryptic Moonglade Park rollout requires an audience to wipe — Wobar doesn't have one to remove. INZO's Overthinker 100M streams was front-loaded by a viral track on a label with full A&R support. **Neither tactic is portable to zero-audience.**

3. **Buffer-style 3-5 posts/week across all channels is not what these artists actually do.** Mport is the outlier with heavy multi-platform output; the other five are *concentrated* on 1-2 anchor platforms. The "post everywhere" model is contradicted by the data — **picking an anchor platform and going deep is what the comps actually do.**

4. **TikTok matters less than the doc implied for psychedelic/experimental bass.** Five of six artists have TikTok followings under 5K. Only Mport has built a real TikTok audience, and his content is humor/meme, not production-process. The Phase 1 doc's heavy TikTok emphasis (Buffer's 2-5/week study) needs to be weighted against the genre reality: in experimental bass, **TikTok is one of several channels, not the primary engine.**

5. **The download-gate / Hypeddit funnel is real but not universal.** Only Mport surfaced a confirmed Hypeddit link in this research. Acyan, AYCH, Kaiyo, Luhv, CANVAS — no public Hypeddit footprint surfaced. **The 30-60% conversion claim that Phase 1 flagged as unsourced is even less anchored than expected** — most stage-appropriate comps don't appear to be using download gates as their primary list-build mechanism.

6. **FUXWITHIT and EDM Identity are the two outlets that actually open doors at this stage.** Phase 1 didn't name specific blogs to prioritize. The data here makes it clear: **a FUXWITHIT guest mix + interview is the most repeatable breakout artifact at the 5K-30K band**, and EDM Identity Artist Spotlight is its near-twin.

## Implications for Wobar's stack

Phase 1 surfaced a roughly $50-200/mo tool stack: MailerLite + Hypeddit + Buffer + SubmitHub + Bandzoogle + Viberate + Feature.fm. Phase 2 evidence reorders priorities:

**Tier 1 — what the comps actually use:**
- **SoundCloud (Pro/Artist Pro)** — already in Wobar's stack. The incubator across all six. Keep.
- **Bandcamp** — only Mport surfaced one publicly, but it's the only platform that pays per sale and most aligned with Wobar's psychedelic/scene-adjacent positioning. **Recommend.**
- **A FUXWITHIT-or-EDM-Identity-shaped editorial relationship.** Not a paid tool, but a deliberate cultivation target. **Recommend prioritizing one blog relationship over SubmitHub/Groover spray.**
- **Hypeddit (for download gates)** — confirmed in use by Mport. Keep, but with realistic conversion expectations per Phase 1 (15-25%, not 30-60%).

**Tier 2 — what the comps may use but isn't visibly load-bearing:**
- **SubmitHub** — useful for playlist push but not what got these artists their breakouts.
- **Buffer / scheduling tools** — only if Wobar picks an anchor platform and decides to post there 3+ times/week. If anchor = TikTok, scheduling matters less because TikTok rewards native posting.
- **Linkfire/Feature.fm smart links** — likely fine, but Hypeddit appears more common in this segment.

**Tier 3 — what Phase 1 prioritized but Phase 2 doesn't validate:**
- **MailerLite** — no visible email-list use among the six. Defer until ~10K monthly listeners or until Wobar has a concrete email-driven release rollout planned.
- **Bandzoogle / artist website** — none of the six lead with an artist website. IG bio + Spotify + SoundCloud + Bandcamp = the actual stack. A website is a Tier 2/3 tool at this stage.
- **Viberate paid tier** — useful at agency-rep stage (~30K+ listeners). Below that, the free tier is enough.
- **Buffer at 3+ channels** — premature given the "anchor one platform" pattern.

**Tactical priorities the Phase 2 evidence supports:**
1. **Pick one anchor platform.** SoundCloud is the safest given the data and Wobar's lane. TikTok is the upside lottery if Wobar can match Mport's humor/meme cadence (he explicitly says it's something he has fun with, not labor).
2. **Cultivate one blog relationship to "guest mix + interview" level.** FUXWITHIT is the highest-leverage target. Track their guest-mix submission pipeline. EDM Identity's Artist Spotlight as backup.
3. **Build a flips library.** Three of six artists used recognizable-IP remixes to drive early traction. Wobar's catalog should include at least 1-2 flips of psychedelic-adjacent IP (Tame Impala? King Gizzard? Floating Points?) per release window.
4. **Set the 10K monthly listener target at 18-30 months from first release**, not 6-12. The data does not support a faster realistic curve at this lane.
5. **Defer the email list until a clear use case emerges.** This contradicts Phase 1 but matches what the comps actually do.

## What we couldn't verify
