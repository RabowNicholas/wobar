# FORMAT TESTING

Formats in this doc are experiments. They exist alongside the three locked formats (The Mirror, The Room, The Transmission) but are not part of the content system until they earn it.

---

## FORMAT 4 — THE CUT

**Bucket:** Mirror
**Asset:** Found footage cold open + visualizer
**Construction:** Scroll-stopping found footage plays for 1–3 seconds. Cut happens at the drop. Visualizer takes over for the remainder. The footage does the stopping. The music does the keeping.

**Hook footage categories (confirmed for testing):**

**Skateboarding** — Street clips, raw filmer energy. Bails and lands both work. The commitment is the thing.

**Nature "What Is Going On"** — Natural disasters, extreme weather, bizarre natural phenomena. Tornados forming, lightning at impossible scale, volcanic eruptions, bioluminescent waves, sinkholes opening. Chaos and scale that makes the drop feel like the soundtrack.

**Extreme Commitment Sports** — Cliff jumping, skydiving, snowboarding. The fully-sent moment. The seconds before impact or flight where there's no turning back.

**Sourcing rules:**
- Found footage only. Not produced, not staged.
- Must feel like someone captured something real.
- Reuse rights need to be confirmed before posting — screen-recorded viral clips will get flagged. Look for Creative Commons, repost accounts with open permissions, or direct outreach to original creators.
- The hook and the visualizer should feel like they belong in the same emotional world even though they're different content. The cut should feel like an escalation, not a topic change.

**What to track:**
- 3-second retention rate (did the hook stop the scroll?)
- Watch-through past the cut (did the music keep them?)
- Which hook category performs best?
- Comment sentiment — are people talking about the music or only the hook footage?

**Kill criteria:** If comments are only about the hook footage and nobody engages with the music, the format is attracting the wrong audience. Adjust or kill.

## FORMAT 5 — THE STACK (You Are Here)

**Bucket:** Mirror / Room
**Asset:** Three horizontal video strips stacked vertically (fills vertical screen)
**Construction:** Three simultaneous perspectives of the same moment from a live set. All three strips play in sync. The viewer feels surrounded.

**Perspective combinations:**
- Drone/wide angle + crowd level + performer close-up
- Wide venue shot + visuals/screen + crowd reaction
- Any three angles that create spatial depth — the point is "you are standing here"

**Best suited for:** Outdoor sets, events with multiple camera angles, sets where drone footage exists. Stronger when there's visual variety between the three strips.

**Sourcing rules:**
- All footage from the same set, same moment.
- Sync must be tight — even a half-second drift breaks the illusion.
- Audio comes from one source (board mix or room mic), not layered.

**What to track:**
- Does the three-strip layout read on small screens or does it feel cramped?
- Watch time vs single-angle set clips
- Save rate (proof-of-experience content should drive saves)
- Do people ask "where was this" or "when's the next one"?

**Kill criteria:** If the format reads as cluttered on phone screens or people don't register that it's three perspectives of the same moment, simplify to two strips or kill.
