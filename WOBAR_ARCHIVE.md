# WOBAR ARCHIVE SOURCING GUIDE

## The Portal Principle
Archive footage is raw material — not finished content. The portal is the constant. What changes is depth of view, type of source material, and degree of processing. Emotional act identity emerges from the intersection of all three.

**Primary source:** Internet Archive (archive.org)
**Sort strategy:** Ascending views to surface less culturally exhausted footage.

---

## ACT 1: RIFT — Container Creation
**Emotional register:** "I am safe here. I can explore. Something is beginning."

**Source Categories**
- Home movies & family footage — domestic life from the 1940s–60s, children playing, ordinary rituals. Familiarity activates safety.
- Pastoral nature footage — open fields, slow rivers, canopy light through trees, dawn landscapes. Nothing threatening.
- Early morning / golden hour film — analog grain in morning light carries a gentle, pre-journey feeling.
- Amateur astronomy films — wide, slow pans across night skies. The cosmos framed as wonder rather than threat.

**Portal Depth**
- Minimum processing. Source material barely touched.
- Gentle desaturation into palette — deep purple undertones applied, black background established.
- Slow breath rhythm — movement slowed to 60–80 BPM equivalent.
- Circle as primary form — footage cropped or framed inside a circle or soft vignette.
- Grain preserved and emphasized.

**What this is NOT:** Do not use footage implying danger, institutional coldness, or mechanical processes.

---

## TRANSITION: RIFT → DESCENSION
**Emotional register:** "Something is pulling. I don't know where yet. But I'm following."

**Source Categories**
- Same categories as Act 1 — the transition is the same source seen differently.
- Nature at the threshold — forests where light is fading, water surfaces that are both mirror and depth.
- Cloud & fog footage — obscures the familiar without replacing it.

**Portal Depth**
- Symmetry begins breaking — the circle from Act 1 starts to distort, 95% perfect but something slightly wrong.
- First mirror distortion — source footage gently mirrored.
- Depth starts forming — flat circle begins to develop a pull toward centre.
- Colour begins shifting — warm tones cool slightly, purple deepens.

---

## ACT 2: DESCENSION — Journey Into Shadow
**Emotional register:** "I'm being pulled somewhere. There's something I need to see."

**Source Categories**
- Nature in motion — underwater footage, root systems, mycelium networks, time-lapse of decay and growth.
- Scientific & industrial processes — mid-century educational films showing fluid dynamics, chemical reactions. Something pulling through a system.
- Cave & subterranean footage — descending into the earth, geological formations, deep water.
- Microscopy & cellular film — the interior of living things. Body-as-landscape footage from 1950s–60s medical films.

**Portal Depth**
- Spiral geometry emerging — Fibonacci proportions guide the form.
- Feedback loops beginning — gentle recursion, image contains itself at decreasing scale.
- Colour descends — deep purple dominant, hints of muted cyan.
- Audio-reactivity increases — sub-bass creates slow massive motion, all movement pulls toward centre.

**What this is NOT:** Do not use confrontational or disorienting footage. Descension pulls — it does not push.

---

## TRANSITION: DESCENSION → ENCOUNTER
**Emotional register:** "Something is here. I can't look away. I don't know what it is yet."

**Source Categories**
- Nature at its most ambiguous — dense underwater footage where scale is lost.
- Industrial machinery — close up. Mid-century industrial films. Grinding, repetitive, relentless.
- Biological processes — visceral. Medical and scientific footage of cellular processes, growth, division.

**Portal Depth**
- Spiral tightening — moves faster, still a spiral, pull is stronger.
- Symmetry at 85% — mirror distortion more pronounced.
- Colour darkens — muted magenta emerges as first sign of confrontation energy.
- Motion becomes slightly unpredictable — occasional interruption, a frame that stutters, a brief glitch.

---

## ACT 3: ENCOUNTER — Confrontation / Recognition
**Emotional register:** "What is this? That's MY shadow. Oh fuck, I see it now."

**Source Categories**
- Medical & scientific footage — X-rays, surgical films, microscopy at confrontational scale. 1950s–60s medical education films. Clinical coldness serves confrontation.
- Industrial machinery — relentless. Factory floor footage, mechanical processes that grind without pause. Inexorable.
- Early psychology & behavioural science — mid-century educational films about the mind, behaviour, social conditioning. Institutional psychology watches, classifies, judges.
- Oscilloscope & wave physics — **Bell Labs and university science films** showing wave interference and resonance. Abstract patterns simultaneously mathematical and visceral. Richest vein for Act 3/4 inflection point.

**Portal Depth**
- Tunnel — full immersion. The spiral has become a tunnel. Infinite depth implied. No visible exit.
- Mirror distortion at maximum — 85–90% symmetry with meaningful asymmetry.
- Impossible geometry peaks — feedback loops create recursive structures.
- Audio-reactivity is aggressive — bass hits create significant visual disruption. No warm colours anywhere.

**What this is NOT:** No emotional relief. No warm or harmonic colour. This act does not comfort.

---

## TRANSITION: ENCOUNTER → RELEASE
**Emotional register:** "I see it. Now I need to move it. Something is about to shift."

**Source Categories**
- Water in motion — breakthrough. Waterfalls, rivers in flood, waves breaking. Not the still mirror of descent — the force of release.
- Crowd & collective body footage — home movies and documentary footage of people in collective movement.
- Nature releasing — time-lapse of flowers opening, pressure systems breaking, storms clearing to light.

**Portal Depth**
- Tunnel begins expanding — direction of visual movement reverses, portal pushes out.
- Symmetry stabilises — mirror distortion reduces.
- Colour begins warming — first reintroduction of warmer tones, muted orange entering palette.
- Rhythm becomes grounded — aggressive audio-reactivity gives way to groove.

---

## ACT 4: RELEASE — Cathartic Discharge
**Emotional register:** "I need to MOVE this. Get it OUT. YES. FUCK YES."

**Source Categories**
- Water — all forms of release. Waterfalls, rivers breaking, ocean waves, rain, flooding systems.
- Collective human movement — festival footage, dance footage, crowd energy from mid-century events.
- Natural force footage — storms, wind in forests, fire (controlled), volcanic activity at distance.
- Space & cosmic footage — early NASA and astronomical footage. After the confrontation of Act 3, the cosmos provides perspective.

**Portal Depth**
- Explosion and expansion — movement now outward, radiating from centre.
- Full colour palette activated — all accents present simultaneously for the first time.
- Maximum audio-reactivity — drops cause full visual climaxes.
- Symmetry returns amplified — the reflection is now a celebration rather than confrontation.

**What this is NOT:** Not confrontational. Not disorienting. Heavy but held. Catharsis has rhythm — chaos does not.

---

## TRANSITION: RELEASE → INTEGRATION
**Emotional register:** "Something has moved. I don't know what it was yet. But I'm different."

**Source Categories**
- Nature after the storm — still water returning after movement, landscapes after rain.
- Slow human footage — home movies at their quietest, people resting, children sleeping.
- Returning light — footage of dawn, clearing fog, light returning to a space.

**Portal Depth**
- Visual energy slowing — motion becomes gentle.
- Colour simplifying — full palette of Act 4 resolves back toward purple and black.
- Form becoming circular again — soft, circular containment. Portal beginning to close.
- Grain returning — rough, matte texture of analog source becomes prominent again, as in Act 1.

---

## ACT 5: INTEGRATION + EMBODIMENT — Grounded Return
**Emotional register:** "What does this mean? I understand now. I'm back. I'm safe. I'm complete."

**Source Categories**
- Return to home movie footage — same categories as Act 1. The same footage carries different weight on return.
- Landscape at rest — nature at its most still. Spaces that invite presence rather than movement.
- Hands & making footage — mid-century footage of craft, making, hands working with material.
- Children & growth footage — not for sentimentality but for the quality of new beginning. Something has just started.

**Portal Depth**
- Portal closing — circle returns. The Wobar logo form returns as the container.
- Minimum processing — mirrors Act 1. The footage is allowed to speak.
- Circular narrative visual callback — use footage from same source category as Act 1 where possible.
- Grain and warmth return — full analog grain, warm undertones return to the purple.

**What this is NOT:** Not passive comedown. This is active integration. The portal closes with intention.

---

## Dither Placement
Belongs in Acts 2–3 (peak at the Act 2→3 transition). Digital/confrontational quality distinguishes it from Act 1's preferred analog film grain.

## Act 5 / Act 1 Callback Rule
Act 5 footage should intentionally mirror Act 1 source categories to create narrative callbacks. The viewer began here. They return here. But the footage that was simply familiar at the opening now carries everything that has happened between.
