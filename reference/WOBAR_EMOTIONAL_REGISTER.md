# WOBAR EMOTIONAL REGISTER

The on-brand check oracle.

When a visual is being made or reviewed, the question is not "does it use the right palette" or "does it use the right primitive." The question is: **does the visual emotionally align with the act this track lives in?**

This document holds the emotional register for each of the 5 acts and the 4 cusps between them. It is purely emotional — no palettes, no operators, no build recipes. Implementation lives in `WOBAR_TD_AGENT_RULES.md`, `WOBAR_TD_REFERENCE.md`, and `WOBAR_GLSL_PATTERNS.md`. The brand-check happens here.

**How to use:**
- During a session, when uncertain whether a visual is on brand, locate the act (or cusp) the track belongs to and check the visual against the **felt sense**, **posture**, **implicit ask**, **quality of movement**, and **what breaks it**.
- The **on-brand check** is the final test: if all five elements line up, the visual is brand-aligned. If any one is off, it's worth asking why.
- Most tracks live on a cusp. The cusps hold two registers at once and have their own logic — read both the cusp entry AND the two acts on either side.

---

## ACT 1 — RIFT

**Felt sense:** Gentle swaying. Eyes closed. The first 30 seconds asks the listener to pay attention to their body and their mind, to find the groove or pocket of the drums.

**Posture:** The dance between WOBAR and the crowd. Some are ready immediately. Others will take all of Act 1 to understand that this is safe. Both are honored.

**Implicit promise:** *You are safe here.*

**Quality of movement:** Sub-perceptual. There is no single moment where you notice the visual shifting — but by the end of the song, you are somewhere different than where you started. Imperceptible drift, not transformation.

**What breaks it:** Aggression. Fast pacing. Anything that overwhelms before they're ready. If the body BRACES instead of softens, the act has failed.

**On-brand check:** The visual invites a soft exhale. The body sways before the mind decides. The listener feels safe enough to close their eyes.

---

## ACT 2 — DESCENSION

**Felt sense:** Hypnotic grinding. Repetition with micro-variations. The head swaying becomes head bobbing, and the eyes open.

**Posture:** Surrender. The listener was being held in safety; now they are being asked to follow. They lean in, willingly, into the pull.

**Implicit ask:** *Follow me.*

**Quality of movement:** Hypnotic. The form persists through the entire visual. It doesn't transform — it BREATHES with the sound. Repetition with micro-variations, not novelty.

**What breaks it:** Loss of the hypnotic nature. Form changes that aren't tied to sound. New shapes appearing instead of the existing form responding. Anything that pulls the listener UP and OUT instead of deeper IN.

**On-brand check:** The form is the same form throughout. It moves WITH the sound, not against or beside it. The listener follows without thinking about whether they want to.

---

## ACT 3 — ENCOUNTER

**Felt sense:** Uncomfortable. Grotesque in the way that makes your skin crawl but you can't look away. Likely encountering yourself — your shadow, your trauma. The body is still, but everything inside is moving.

**Posture:** Witnessing. The listener may not consciously see what's there, but they FEEL it. This act is somatic. The visual identity must follow suit — not explain, not narrate, just hold.

**Implicit ask:** *Sit with this. Sit through this.* Patience is the discipline.

**Quality of movement:** Tension and small releases. The same architecture as the music — tritone and half-step relationships, dissonance that resolves only briefly before the next dissonance. Intentional forms with organized chaos. Held, not chaotic.

**What breaks it:** Relieving the discomfort before it's done its work. Too many words or melodies. Visual changes that pull the listener out of the somatic into the intellectual. Anything that makes the experience watchable instead of felt. The tension must be sustained for long enough to do its work — premature release is failure.

**On-brand check:** The visual is HARD to look at, in the way mirrors are hard. The listener stays in their body even while wanting to leave. The form holds tension the way the music does — intentional, organized, not random.

---

## ACT 4 — RELEASE

**Felt sense:** Variable per listener, per day. After the deep encounter, the body craves something. It needs to be listened to and taken care of. Most often this means dancing — moving to release whatever was held.

**Posture:** Discharge. The tension is leaving — whatever the ego was blocking from being seen. Sometimes the relationship with the ego itself. This maps directly to the catharsis of shadow work. Individual, but archetypally consistent.

**Implicit ask:** *Move.*

**Quality of movement:** Driving. Where Act 2 was hypnotic, Act 4 is the same skeleton but propulsive. Groove, rhythm, forward motion. The body propelled instead of pulled.

**What breaks it:** Too slow. Not groovy enough. No rhythm. Anything hypnotic instead of driving — that's Act 2's job. Anything stuck or sluggish — the body cannot discharge in still water.

**On-brand check:** The body is moving. Visibly, ungovernably. The visual has rhythm and forward propulsion. If Act 2's mirror is "follow me" and Act 4's mirror is "move," the visual makes the latter feel inevitable.

---

## ACT 5 — INTEGRATION + EMBODIMENT

**Felt sense:** Settling. The body is ready to ground itself. The journey just happened — now there's space to hold it, decide what it means, and feel whatever emotions surface.

**Posture:** Integration. The listener is metabolizing the experience. They may or may not consciously recognize what happened — but they're being prepared to recognize it later, when the moment matches.

**Implicit ask:** *Feel.*

**Quality of movement:** Slow, deliberate. The listener needs to walk through this slowly to get what they need from it. Pacing is the discipline — there's no rush, and there shouldn't be.

**What breaks it:** Too fast. Anything that interrupts the slow walk through. Anything that pulls the listener back into Act 4's discharge or jolts them with a new peak. Integration cannot be rushed.

**On-brand check:** The listener can hear themselves think again, but isn't pushed to. The visual moves at the pace of an exhale. If they wanted to walk away, they could — but they don't, because the slowness is doing something they need.

# CUSPS

Most tracks live here. The cusps are slow arcs, not hard cuts — the lines between acts are blurry. A cusp holds two registers at once, and the visual must hold both.

---

## CUSP — ACT 1 → ACT 2 (safe → follow)

**Carried from Act 1:** Safety, but as a remembered quality rather than a present one. As the hypnotic grinding emerges, some listeners start to feel uncomfortable. The cusp's job is to reassure — *the safety still holds while you're pulled deeper.*

**Stirring from Act 2:** The hypnotic drive. Micro-variations. More psychedelic texture. Structure beginning to emerge underneath.

**What lives only here:** *Remember you are safe to let go.* Not "you are safe" alone, not "follow me" alone. The two-tone field where both are present and the listener is given permission for the descent.

**What breaks it:** Hypnotic elements arriving too quickly. Too much energy. A big drop. Anything that turns the slow arc into a hard cut.

---

## CUSP — ACT 2 → ACT 3 (follow → sit with)

**Carried from Act 2:** The descent continues, but it's different now. The organizing hypnotic pull has become more organic and less structured. There is a distinct marking moment here — a felt threshold that the listener knows they crossed even if they couldn't name it.

**Stirring from Act 3:** The texture and landscape are starting to emerge — but everything is still recognizable. Not yet the full grotesque, but the surfaces are starting to shift.

**What lives only here:** *Anticipation.* The dread before the encounter. The body sensing what's coming before the mind has any words for it.

**What breaks it:** Too sudden a confrontation. The encounter arriving before the anticipation has done its work. The marking moment must be a threshold, not a wall.

---

## CUSP — ACT 3 → ACT 4 (sit with → move)

**Carried from Act 3:** The heavy bass is still present — but instead of disorienting, it's starting to LOCK IN. The same weight, transformed in function: it was crushing, now it's grounding.

**Stirring from Act 4:** Movement and groove slowly arriving. The body is being invited back into rhythm, but tentatively.

**What lives only here:** *Settle into it.* The ask is to begin to settle — find the alignment with the rhythm that's emerging, let the held weight become forward motion. Neither full encounter nor full release.

**What breaks it:** Too dancy too quickly. Too groovy too soon. Vocals arriving early. Anything that skips the locking-in phase and jumps straight to discharge — premature catharsis is hollow.

---

## CUSP — ACT 4 → ACT 5 (move → feel)

**Carried from Act 4:** The emotional core is the lingering quality — but it shifts as the heaviness is stripped away. The body still has motion in it, but the discharge is winding down.

**Stirring from Act 5:** Heavy melodic drops with lots of emotion built in. Pre-stillness — the drops aren't asking the body to discharge anymore, they're asking it to FEEL.

**What lives only here:** *Heavy and beautiful.* This is where the hard-won softness lives — the heart-space opening after the cathartic discharge. Neither full release nor full integration. The body is cooling down while the heart is opening.

**What breaks it:** Jumping into integration too quickly. The body needs time to cool down. Stripping the heaviness before it's been honored, or pulling the listener into stillness before they've ridden the residual motion through.

# Using this document

When making or reviewing a visual:

1. Identify the act (or cusp) the track lives in. Music dictates this — the BPM, the energy arc, what the song is asking the listener to do.
2. Read the relevant entry. Hold all five elements in mind: **felt sense**, **posture**, **implicit ask**, **quality of movement**, **what breaks it**.
3. Check the visual against the **on-brand check**.
4. If the visual is missing one or two elements, identify which and decide whether to fix it or accept the deviation deliberately. If three or more are off, the visual likely belongs to a different act.
5. Cusp tracks: read the cusp AND both adjacent acts. The cusp's "what lives only here" is usually the load-bearing element.

The implementation details (palettes, ops, primitives, build recipes) live elsewhere. What you bring HERE is the question: does this feel like the act this track lives in?

If yes — ship it. If no — adjust until yes.
