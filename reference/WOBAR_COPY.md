# WOBAR COPY STANDARDS

## Core Tension
Every piece of Wobar copy must hold: heavy AND transformative. Filthy AND sacred. Dark AND luminous. This is not contradiction. This is the point. Wobar has teeth, not just vibes — but those teeth serve revelation, not chaos.

---

## Archetype Split in Copy

| | THE MAGICIAN (70%) | THE SAGE (30%) |
|--|---|---|
| Mode | Creates conditions for transformation | Names what is already true |
| Voice | Speaks from action and experience | Speaks from observation and revelation |
| Method | Invites, doesn't explain | Provokes, doesn't lecture |
| Language | Sensory, physical, sonic | Philosophical, paradoxical |
| Example | "Surrender to the frequency" | "Bass frequencies don't create the feeling. They reveal what you've been holding." |
| Primary use | Portal Transmission, show copy, track/EP descriptions | Revelations/Transmissions, bio endings, caption closers |

**Writing test:** Does this create a portal (Magician) or name a truth (Sage)? If neither, rewrite. If both equally, simplify until one leads.

---

## Voice Attributes (Non-Negotiable)

1. **GROUNDED** — Every transformation claim is anchored in the physical. Bass does something to the body first. That physical truth earns the spiritual language.
2. **PRECISE** — Vague spiritual language is the enemy. Replace "transcendent experience" with what actually happens. Replace "feel the energy" with what the body does.
3. **UNAFRAID OF DARKNESS** — The shadow is the portal. Copy does not soften the confrontational nature of the work. Encounter is uncomfortable by design.
4. **SELF-AWARE** — Wobar sits at a culturally awkward intersection of heavy music and inner work. The voice acknowledges the strangeness with a wry edge.
5. **EARNED** — Every spiritual or transformation claim must be backed by demonstrated practice. Speak from what has actually happened, not what sounds good.

## Sentence-Level Rules

**Length**
- Short declarative for impact: "The bass knows before you do."
- Medium for context and setup
- Long only when following a complex thread — never more than two in a row
- Fragments allowed when intentional: "Not entertainment. A journey."

**Rhythm**
- Lead with physical or sonic, land on psychological: "The sub hits your chest before it hits your mind."
- Three-part structure for Transmissions: statement / provocation / landing
- Paradox pairs are native: "The heaviest drop is the softest landing." / "The darkest act is the most liberating."

**Person**
- Second person ("you") is primary — creates intimacy without authority
- First person ("I") only in The Becoming content
- Third person only in official bios
- Never "we" as royal voice. "We" is for community only.

| Never Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|------------|
| Good vibes only | Go through, not around |
| Love and light | The bass reveals what you've been holding |
| Raise your vibration | The drop is the moment of dissolution |
| Sacred journey | Heavy bass with intention behind every drop |
| Quantum healing | A structured journey |
| We are all one | Bass music as portal technology |
| Ego death (as status marker) | The shadow is the portal |
| Higher self / lower self | Surrender to the frequency |
| Trust the Universe | The work is happening whether you know it or not |
| EDM (as genre label for Wobar) | Psychedelic bass |
| Conscious music | There's no escaping yourself — that's the point |
| Spiritual awakening (as marketing promise) | What just happened? |
| Healing frequencies | Integration is the morning after |

**Additional rules:** Never use "I don't have an ego" energy. Never claim transcendence you haven't demonstrated. Never use "ceremony" for a standard club booking. Never make transformation sound like a guaranteed product outcome.

## The Three Brand Writing Tests

Run every piece of copy through these before finalizing.

### TEST 1: THE PORTAL TEST
Does this copy open a portal — create conditions for something to happen in the reader — or does it just describe Wobar? Describing Wobar is not the goal. Creating an experience of Wobar in text is the goal. If it explains rather than opens, rewrite.

### TEST 2: THE BODY TEST
Is at least one claim grounded in the physical? Does the body appear — as sensation, as movement, as response? Transformation language without somatic grounding reads as spiritual bypassing.

### TEST 3: THE EARNED LANGUAGE TEST
Is every spiritual or transformation claim backed by demonstrated practice? The 5-Act Framework is real. The residency is real. Speak from what has actually happened. If the copy makes a claim Wobar cannot yet demonstrate — remove it or soften it.

## Bio Templates

### Short Bio (50 words)
Wobar opens portals through bass music. Dubstep, halftime, drum & bass, and experimental bass structured around the 5-Act Portal Framework — a journey architecture built on shadow work and somatic release. For ravers who want more than bangers. Based in Salt Lake City.

### Medium Bio (100 words)
Wobar opens portals through bass music. Every set is a structured journey built around the 5-Act Portal Framework — a sonic architecture that maps psychedelic bass performance to shadow work methodology. Rift. Descension. Encounter. Release. Integration. In that order. Always. The music moves across dubstep, halftime, drum & bass, midtempo, and experimental bass — unified by psychedelic sound design and the belief that heavy bass is portal technology. That the right frequency at the right time can dissolve the boundary between who you think you are and who you actually are. For ravers who want more than bangers. The journey is the point.

### Long Bio (250 words)
Wobar opens portals through bass music — and means it. Based in Salt Lake City, Wobar holds a monthly residency at Flow State where sets are built not as playlists but as complete journeys. The 5-Act Portal Framework — Rift, Descension, Encounter, Release, Integration — maps every performance to the architecture of authentic inner transformation. Shadow work methodology, IFS therapy principles, and somatic experiencing inform the arc. The bass carries it. The sound moves fluidly across psychedelic dubstep, halftime, drum & bass, midtempo, and experimental bass. Organic textures, spatial width, and evolving sound design in the range of Of The Trees, INZO, and Shlump — heavy where it needs to be heavy, spacious where spaciousness serves the journey. Festival-ready weight with intentional structure. The working thesis: bass music is portal technology. The right frequencies at the right time can dissolve the boundary between who you think you are and who you actually are. Not through escape — through encounter. The framework exists because transformation has a shape, and a set that honors that shape does something different from entertainment. Audiences have described Wobar sets as "taking them somewhere they didn't know they needed to go." Some stay until close to 5am. Some cry during the integration phase. Most say they didn't expect any of it at a bass show. That reaction is the whole point.
